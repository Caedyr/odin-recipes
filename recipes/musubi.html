Spam Musubi

A canned delight!

Yes, really. I mean that. To those who haven't tried it before, spam is hardly an appealing prospect; Its canned meat, looks pale, and has a name commonly associated with unwanted messages nowadays. But hear me out:

Just try it.

I promise you, its pretty good.

Ingredient List

  • One can of spam musubi
  • Six cups of cooked sushi rice
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup mirin
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • Nori roasted seaweed, cut into thirds

Recipe Steps

  1. Cut the spam into four to eight even slices lengthwise, depending on how thick you want it to be.
  2. Set a medium sized, non stick pan to medium heat. Add 1 tsp of olive oil.
  3. Add the spam to the pan for about three minutes, flipping until both sides are slightly golden.
  4. Remove the spam from the pan, placing on paper towers to drain the oil. Clean the pan.
  5. Set the pan on high heat. Add the soy sauce, mirin, and sugar to the pan, and once its all mixed through and begins to simmer, turn the heat low.
  6. Re-add the spam. Turn to coat, and once coated, remove from the heat.
  7. Using either the spam can, or a musubi mold, fill the mold with about an inch of rice on a strip of nori. Remember to keep the mold wet with water to prevent sticking!
  8. Remove the mold, set a slice of spam on top, and wrap the nori around. The nori can be sealed with a droplet of water if needed.
